so, what's mainthread?
Marshaled project context to elucidate mainthread concept

mainthread is a personal productivity platform built around one core idea: you're one person living across multiple contexts — work, family, household — but your tools aren't.

The system is organized around three layers:

  • Swimlanes — major life domains (Work, Family, Household, etc.)

  • Threads — long-running projects within those domains, each with a clear definition of done

  • Tasks — individual units of work inside threads, each with a deadline, effort estimate, and a dynamically recalculated priority score

What makes it different from a typical todo app is the priority engine. Instead of you manually deciding what to work on, mainthread scores every task daily based on six factors: time pressure, consequence of missing it, who's affected, how important the thread is, your own judgment (the "important" flag), and dependency order. Tasks bubble up automatically as deadlines approach.

The end goal is a daily shortlist — a capacity-aware view that tells you exactly what to work on today across all areas of your life, so nothing slips through the cracks whether it's a work deliverable or a bill you need to pay.

Phase 2 of the vision takes it further: rather than just managing tasks yourself, you'd delegate them to AI agents — turning mainthread into a personal delegation layer.

"One Life, One System."
